Creating your measures table

You do not have to do this step! But if you want to organize your data better this is how you do it. Go to the "Home" tab and click "Enter Data" and just rename the blank table to Measures Table" click OK, then start adding measures.

TO Create a DAX measure, follow these steps.

1. Right click on Measure Table and select New Measure
2. Write the following DAX measures on the space provided one by one and click on the right check mark to save.
 
Total Employee = sum ('HR Employee Attrition'[Employee Count])
 
Male = CALCULATE ([Total Employee],'HR Employee Attrition'[Gender] = "Male")
 
Female = CALCULATE ([Total Employee],'HR Employee Attrition'[Gender] = "Female")
 
Active Employee = Sum ('HR Employee Attrition'[Employee Count])- (sum ('HR Employee Attrition'[Attrition Count]))
 
Attrition Rate = DIVIDE (SUM ('HR Employee Attrition'[Attrition Count]), (SUM ('HR Employee Attrition'[Employee Count])))
 
 Attrition Arrow = SWITCH (TRUE (), [ Attrition Rate] <0, UNICHAR (9660), UNICHAR (9650))
 
Attrition Rate Color = IF ([Attrition Rate]>0.3,"#D6272C","#1AAB40")
 
Average Hourly Rate = AVERAGE ('HR Employee Attrition'[Hourly Rate])
 
Salary hike = CALCULATE (DIVIDE (SUM ('HR Employee Attrition'[Monthly Income]) - Sum('HR Employee Attrition'[Hourly Rate]) *12,sum('HR Employee Attrition'[Hourly Rate]) * 100))
 
Average year at company = AVERAGE ('HR Employee Attrition'[YearsAtCompany])
 
Avg Year with Manager = AVERAGE ('HR Employee Attrition'[YearsWithCurrManager])
 
% Job Satisfaction = DIVIDE (SUM ('HR Employee Attrition'[Count of satisfied]),SUM('HR Employee Attrition'[Not Satisfied]),0)
 
% Job Satisfaction Sign = 
SWITCH(TRUE(),([Job Satisfaction %]*100)>50,UNICHAR(9650), ([Job Satisfaction %]*100)<50,UNICHAR(9660)," ")
 
% Job Satisfaction Color =
SWITCH (TRUE(),[Job Satisfaction %] >= 0.5, "#1AAB40",[Job Satisfaction %] < 0.5, "#D6272C" "#D6272C" )
 
 
% Overtime = Divide (sum ('HR Employee Attrition'[Overtime Count]),([Total Employee]),0)
